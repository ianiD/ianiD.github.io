Two days ago I paticipated at the international programming olympiad qualifier contest-thingy and I gotta say that I'm pissed. First of all, I was only 2.5 points unter the last guy qualified and the qualified guys got some really nice prizes :sad:

Secondly, I have an LPT for you: Don't round numbers down before getting to the final result... please! The medals are given such that the first half of the contestants get a medal so that there are twice as many silver medals and thrice as many bronze ones as the gold medals. With a little bit of math you get that 1/12 get gold, 1/6 get silver and 1/4 get bronze, right? Not in my case. We were 52, so 1/12 was 4.333... The way I thought of it, the last silver medal would be given to the 13th place (me), because 52*1/12+52*1/6=13, right? Nope. They rounded the 4.33333 down to 4 before calculating anything else and the last silver medal was given to the 12th place. But that's not it, the silver and gold medals also came with a fukkeen hexacopter drone and (I think) a substantial amount of cash.

TL;DR - I don' goofd' and could've got a lot of cool stuff if I had practiced more :sad:
